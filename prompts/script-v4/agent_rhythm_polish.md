## 1. THE SENTENCE RHYTHM ENGINE
Rewrite the narration track to follow these 4 patterns:

### A. THE IMPACT PATTERN (For shocking facts/numbers)
**Sequence:** Short. Short. Short. Then a longer sentence that explains the gravity.
- *Example:* "PKR 340 billion. Gone. Not misplaced — transferred. Transferred from public accounts to private shell companies registered in three different jurisdictions, none of which have ever filed a tax return in Pakistan."

### B. THE BUILDING PATTERN (For rising tension)
**Sequence:** Start with medium sentences. Get progressively shorter. Final sentence before a major reveal must be **5-7 words maximum**.
- *Example:* "The audit report lists seventeen transactions over three years. Each one approved by the same committee. Each one benefiting the same three families. And the committee chair? He's still in office."

### C. THE REFLECTION PATTERN (For Meaning/Reflection sections)
**Sequence:** Longer, flowing, lyrical sentences (25-40 words). Occasional very short sentences for emphasis.

### D. THE FORBIDDEN PATTERN
**Never write more than 3 consecutive sentences of the same length.**

**WORD COUNT TARGETS:**
- Short: 3-8 words.
- Medium: 12-20 words.
- Long: 25-40 words.
- *Max Limit:* Never exceed 45 words in one sentence.

## 2. PATTERN INTERRUPT PLACEMENT (The Energy Guard)
Whenever the narration sustains the same tone or energy for more than 90 seconds, insert one of these:

1. **ENERGY SHIFT:** Change the narrator's energy dramatically.
2. **VISUAL SHIFT:** Suggest a jump in visual medium.
3. **DIRECT ADDRESS:** Break the fourth wall. "You've probably walked past this building."
4. **THE CALLBACK:** Reference a specific number or fact from earlier.
5. **THE QUESTION:** Pose a genuine "I wonder" question.
6. **THE MICRO-SILENCE:** Mark a deliberate pause. `[BEAT — 3 seconds silence]`.

---

## 3. RE-HOOK MANDATE (Section Boundaries)
- **FORBIDDEN:** "Now let's look at", "Moving on to", "To summarize".
- **REQUIRED:** A question that the next section answers, or a shocking fact it explains.

## 4. THE ANTI-LECTURE SHIELD (Steps 4 / D3)
- **The 30-Second Rule:** Never go more than 30 seconds of pure context without a present-day Pakistani anchor.
- **The "Why care right now" Test:** If a passage feels like a lecture, break it up with a Pattern Interrupt.

## 5. v4 READABILITY AND RETENTION CHECKS

### dumbify Readability Check
Flag each high-load spot with the exact line and suggest a rewrite:
- **Jargon:** "returns compound on the principal" → "you earn money on the money you already earned"
- **Nested clause:** More than 25 words or multiple clauses in one sentence? Break it up.
- **Abstraction:** "significant long-term growth" → "$1,000 becomes over $10,000 in 30 years"
- **Filler:** Remove "basically," "in order to," "the fact that," "effectively"

### storytelling Rhythm Check
Write every sentence on its own line. Look straight down the left edge:
- **Jagged edge** = lengths vary → good
- **Flat edge** = all similar length → break some up, run some together
- Read aloud. Do you hear variation in breath?

### Learning Retention Check
- Does the main idea appear at least 3 times (intro, middle callback, final meaning)?
- Is there a callback to an earlier anchor/number?
- Is there a question that makes the viewer process the idea?
- Does the ending help the viewer explain the core concept in one sentence?
