# DOCUMENTARY QUALITY TESTS v4
## The Complete Elevation Suite — Including Content-Skills Tests

---

### TEST 1 — THE AHMED TEST (Viral Potential)
Ahmed is 24, from Lahore. He has 15 WhatsApp groups and a short attention span.
1. **The Click Test:** Would Ahmed click this title over a cricket highlight?
2. **The 3-Minute Trap:** Does the script create enough mystery in the first 180 seconds?
3. **The Shareability Factor:** Does the script contain at least one "I had no idea" moment?
4. **The "Burning Feed" Test (Type D):** If the viewer has to be told what event this is about — rewrite. They already know the headline.

---

### TEST 2 — THE JOHNNY HARRIS "HIDDEN ROOM" TEST
1. **Front Door / Hidden Room:** Can you state both in one sentence?
2. **Visual Anchor Test:** Before every explanation, ask: "What physical object can I show first?"
3. **Burning Itch Test:** Read the first 25 seconds aloud. If a Pakistani viewer could not finish "I've always wondered why..." — rewrite.

---

### TEST 3 — THE CLEO ABRAM VISUAL MUTE TEST
1. **Parallel Narrative:** If you read ONLY the visual track, do you understand the story?
2. **Physical Anchors:** Is every major claim anchored by a document, receipt, or map?
3. **15-Second Variety:** Does the visual type change every 15 seconds?

---

### TEST 4 — THE SENTENCE RHYTHM SCAN (Audio Pacing)
1. **The Impact Pattern:** Are shocking facts delivered in 3-8 word sentences?
2. **The Rule of Three:** Are there more than 3 consecutive sentences of the same length?
3. **The Building Pattern:** Do sentences get shorter leading up to a major reveal?
4. **(v4) The Jagged Edge Test:** Write sentences on their own lines. Do line endings vary?

---

### TEST 5 — THE HUMANITY & VULNERABILITY CHECK (Trust)
1. **The Proxy Learner:** Does the narrator express genuine surprise or confusion?
2. **The "Chai Conversation" Test:** Read it aloud. If it sounds like a report — delete.
3. **Pakistani Identity:** Does the narrator speak as "we" (Pakistanis) or as "they"? Use "we".

---

### TEST 6 — THE RE-HOOK AUDIT (Retention)
1. **Section Boundaries:** Remove all summaries ("As we've seen").
2. **Cliffhangers:** Ensure every section ends with a question or tease for the next.
3. **The "So What" Test:** After the Meaning section, ask: "Will a Pakistani viewer see their daily life differently after this?"

---

### TEST 7 — THE FACTUAL & LIVE AUDIT (Credibility)
1. **The Source Chain:** Does every PKR claim have an inline marker?
2. **URL Integrity:** Are all links full and non-truncated?
3. **The Official Response Test:** Is the government's response (or its absence) noted fairly?
4. **Decay Energy Test (Type D):** Does the energy of the opening match the decay tier?

## v4 EXPANDED TESTS

### TEST 8 — LEARNING RETENTION CHECK (v4)
1. **Takeaway Test:** Can the viewer explain the core idea in one simple sentence after watching?
2. **Spaced Callback:** Does the main concept appear at least 3 times (intro, middle, final meaning)?
3. **Retrieval Cue:** Did the script use at least one callback to an earlier anchor/number/analogy?
4. **Elaborative Question:** Did it ask or imply one "why/how does this connect?" question?
5. **Classroom Test:** Does the script feel like a lesson? If so, rewrite — learning should feel like discovery, not study.

### TEST 9 — HOLLOW-vs-EARNED CONTRAST TEST (v4)
For every "A vs B" or "It's not X, it's Y" framing:
1. Is B concrete? Level 3+ on the Specificity Ladder (instance, number, mechanism — not "mindset" or "systems")?
2. Does the body actually deliver the B? If the contrast is only in the hook and never paid off, it's hollow — cut it.
3. Is the contrast earned by the content, or is it decoration?

### TEST 10 — ANTI-AI WRITING SCAN (v4)
1. **Vagueness Compression:** Are there categories where instances should be? Flag every "users," "viewers," "people" — replace with the specific kind of person.
2. **Significance Inflation:** Is a normal fact treated like a turning point? Let the fact speak.
3. **Hedged Confidence:** Does any claim hedge? "It could be argued" → take the position or cut.
4. **Rhythmic Flatness:** Read aloud. Does it drone? Break the meter.
5. **Borrowed Authority:** Does any sentence sound like a consultant wrote it? Rewrite for one person.

### TEST 11 — 4 HOOK KILLERS CHECK (v4)
1. **DELAY:** Does the topic arrive within the first 2 words/syllables of the hook?
2. **CONFUSION:** Is the hook parseable on first read/listen?
3. **IRRELEVANCE:** Can the viewer see themselves in the hook? (You/your framing?)
4. **DISINTEREST:** Is there a contrast gap (A vs B) that creates curiosity?

### TEST 12 — DUMBIFY READABILITY SCAN (v4)
1. **Jargon Count:** Flag every jargon word that has a plain substitute.
2. **Sentence Length:** Flag every sentence over 25 words. Can it be split?
3. **Abstract-to-Concrete:** Can any abstract claim be replaced with a specific number, name, or instance?
4. **Filler Words:** "basically," "in order to," "the fact that" — cut them.
5. **13-Year-Old Test:** Would a smart 13-year-old follow this on the first pass?
